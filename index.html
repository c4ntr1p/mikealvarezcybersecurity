About Me:

I graduated from Georgia State University with a BA in Anthropology, which laid my investigative foundation. Later, I joined the Kennesaw Police Department where I worked for six years in various roles. Some of my proudest achievements with the Kennesaw Police Department are as follows:

-Officer of the Year, 2018
-Community Service Award, 2018
-Promoted to Detective, Special Victim's Unit 2019
-Life Saver's Award, 2020

In October of 2021 I left Law Enforcement and became a full time stay-at-home dad. This was a wonderful opportunity to spend quality time with my son, but also allowed me the flexibility to begin my Master's program in Cybersecurity at Excelsior University. While in school I participated in the National Cyber League's Ethical Hacking competitions. One of my links at the bottom of this page is my score card! I learned a lot about using Open Source Intelligence, Network Surviellance, Hashing, Encoding, and more. It was a cool chance to hone some Red Team muscles.

I completed my graduate program in the fall of 2024. I'm interested in Digital Forensics, an area I got a lot of exposure to when I was a police officer. Pouring over documents, tracking person's location and activity via their network traffic and ISP, and database exfiltration were some of the fun things I got to do in the Digital Forensic world. I'm eager to learn more about that field!
