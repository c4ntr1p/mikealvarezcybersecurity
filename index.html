About Me:

I'm a passionate and dedicated worker, and if you'll indulge me I'd like to share my background with you. I graduated from Georgia State University with a BA in Anthropology, which laid my investigative foundation. Later, I joined the Kennesaw Police Department where I worked for six years in various roles. Some of my proudest achievements with the Kennesaw Police Department are as follows:

-Officer of the Year, 2018
-Community Service Award, 2018
-Promoted to Detective, Special Victim's Unit 2019
-Life Saver's Award, 2020

In October of 2021 I left Law Enforcement and became a full time stay-at-home dad. This was a wonderful opportunity to spend quality time with my son, but also allowed me the flexibility to begin my Master's program in Cybersecurity at Excelsior University. While in school I participated in the National Cyber League's Ethical Hacking competitions. I learned a lot about using Open Source Intelligence, Network Surviellance, Hashing, Encoding, and more. It was a cool chance to hone some Red Team muscles.

I've been diving into mini-projects, which has been awesome because I love learning new things and exploring technology. Whether it's the practical applications of Offensive Security strategies I've learned using the game-ified virtual machine 'GreyHack', setting up a TCP server on my Raspberry Pi to send messages between it and my homelab desktop, or just learning the flexibility of the Python language; every day's incredibly exciting!
